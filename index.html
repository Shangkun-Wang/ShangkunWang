    Research Experience

    Motion state of stable droplet actuated by a piezoelectric nozzle

    2018.06-Present

    Advisor: Prof. Haisheng Fang, Multiscale Process Modeling Lab (MPML), HUST

    Background: Piezoelectric inkjet printing is a technology widely used in display, electronic and life science markets. Most studies have focused on the acoustic characteristics of the pressure channel or the jet stability but little attention has been paid to the motion states of the single droplet, which have great effects when it impact on substrates.

    My work:

  • Enhanced the performance of the inkjet Microdispensing Platform and carried out systematic experiments capturing images of single droplet at different operating conditions by CCD camera

  • Used level set method to simulate drop formation and oscillation processes to get details of drops’ motion states

  • Wrote MATLAB code to draw image information and process data

  • Analyzed and interpreted the experimental and simulated results using dimensional analysis

  • Independently preparing a paper based on above research for submission to an academic journal as first author


    Influence of second air (SA) distribution on co-firing sludge in a pulverized coal-fired utility boiler

    2018.04-2018.09

    Advisor: Associate Prof. Qingyan Fang, the State Key Laboratory of Coal Combustion (SKLCC), HUST

    Background: Sludge incineration is a promising method to achieve thorough, innocuous and economical sludge disposal. However considering the unique characteristics of sludge with high nitrogen and moisture content, it is vital to investigate how the aerodynamic field and oxygen-feed in the boiler should be modified to maximize the efficiency and minimize the pollutants emission.

    My work:

  • Designed several sets of computational conditions to comprehensively studied the influence of air distribution on sludge co- combustion

  • Used ANSYS Fluent to simulate combustion in a full-scale 100 MW boiler model and obtained data of temperature, heat flux, burnout rate, CO, and NOx for further assess.

  • Proposed the optimal SA distribution after taking into account multiple factors such as boiler efficiency and environmental effects

  • Finished a paper for submission to an academic journal


    High throughput spray coating of membranes for applications in desalination and waste chemical recovery

    2017.07-2017.08

    Advisor: Associate Prof. David B. Go, Associate Prof. William Phillip, Department of Aerospace and Mechanical engineering, University of Notre Dame

    Background: The efficient separation of metal ions from aqueous solution is important for public health and the environment. Electrospray is a promising method to achieve high throughput spray coating of nanostructured membranes which can absorb ions selectively. However to optimize the manufacturing process, various working conditions and the solution chemistry should be

    tested.

    My work:

  • Helped rebuild the piezoelectric-based spray system

  • Conducted spray experiments on a traditional electrospray system and a piezoelectric-based spray system

  • Characterized the hydraulic permeability and copper-capture ability of membranes by a circular hole punch and UV-Vis

  • Wrote an instruction on the samples for later researches
